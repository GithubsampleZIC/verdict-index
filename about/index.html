About VERDICT

VERDICT is an independent evaluation index that scores AI agent and workflow automation platforms on security, privacy, and compliance. We use public data only. We accept zero vendor revenue. We publish scores by name.

Why VERDICT Exists

AI agents are moving into production across enterprises. They connect to email, CRM, code repositories, and customer data. Procurement teams evaluating these tools face a problem: compliance badges do not reliably predict security posture.

A platform can hold SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certifications and still have unpatched critical vulnerabilities, permissive data training policies, or no vulnerability disclosure program. VERDICT exists to surface these gaps using a consistent, transparent framework applied equally to every platform regardless of size, funding, or market position.

No other public index scores specific AI agent platforms by name with a published methodology. VERDICT fills that gap.

Independence Model

01Zero vendor revenue. No platform under evaluation has ever paid VERDICT. No platform ever will.
02No paid certifications or badges. VERDICT does not sell "certified" or "approved" designations.
03No pre-publication sharing. Vendors are notified after publication, not before. Corrections are invited with supporting documentation.
04Public data only. Layer 0 evaluations use CVE databases, CISA KEV, official documentation, Terms of Service, and compliance certifications. No vendor accounts or internal access.
05Silence is a data point. If a platform does not publicly document a security control, it is scored as absent.

How Evaluations Work

Each platform is scored under VERDICT Framework v0.3.1 across seven dimensions: Verifiability, Effectiveness, Resilience, Data Conduct, Identity & Control, Containment, and Transparency. Layer 0 evaluations use public documentation only and score a maximum of 85 points (the Effectiveness dimension is reserved for Layer 1 behavioral testing).

Every evaluation includes positive findings alongside recorded concerns. Scores are risk factor records, not pass/fail judgments. VERDICT describes what the public data shows and leaves interpretation to the reader.

After publication, vendors receive a notification email inviting factual corrections with supporting public documentation. Corrections are verified and applied promptly. One score correction has been applied through this process to date.

Full methodology details are available on the Methodology page.

Who Operates VERDICT

VERDICT is operated by ZinovaCreation, based in Japan. The project is led by Tatsuya Suzuki.

Evaluation tooling uses Claude (Anthropic). This relationship is disclosed in every evaluation report. Anthropic is an AI company that competes with several evaluated vendors, and Amazon (whose platforms score highest in the index) is a major Anthropic investor. VERDICT applies identical evaluation criteria to all platforms regardless of their relationship to Anthropic.

Standing Bias Disclosure
VERDICT evaluations are conducted using Claude (Anthropic) as tooling. Anthropic competes in the AI market with Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Salesforce, and others whose products are evaluated in this index. Amazon, whose Amazon Q Business holds the highest VERDICT score, is a major investor in Anthropic. VERDICT discloses these relationships in every evaluation report and applies identical scoring criteria, evidence standards, and evaluation methodology to all platforms. No scoring adjustments are made based on any vendor's relationship to Anthropic.
