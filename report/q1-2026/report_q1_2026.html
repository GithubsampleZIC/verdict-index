Executive Summary

VERDICT evaluated 20 AI agent and workflow automation platforms on security, privacy, and compliance during Q1 2026. All evaluations used publicly available information only (Layer 0), under Framework v0.3.1. No vendor was contacted prior to publication. No vendor revenue was accepted.

Scores ranged from 30 to 65 out of a maximum 85 points. The mean score was 48.1; the median was 47. The highest-scoring platform, Vertex AI Agent Builder, achieved 65/85 — 76.5% of the theoretical maximum. No platform reached 80%.

Two platforms are listed in the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog: n8n (CVE-2025-68613) and Langflow (CVE-2025-3248). Both have confirmed active exploitation in the wild.

Compliance certifications do not reliably predict security posture. Platforms holding SOC 2, ISO 27001, and other certifications experienced critical vulnerabilities and supply chain compromises at rates indistinguishable from uncertified platforms.

This finding arrives at a moment of rapid, ungoverned deployment. According to Gravitee's 2026 survey of 900+ practitioners, 80.9% of technical teams have moved AI agents into production — but only 14.4% did so with full security and IT approval. The gap between adoption speed and security readiness defines the current landscape.

On March 24, 2026, LiteLLM — a library holding SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certifications — was compromised through its PyPI distribution. Approximately 47,000 downloads occurred within a three-hour attack window. The certifications provided no early warning. VERDICT had flagged the structural risk of unpinned dependencies across this index before the incident occurred.

Score Distribution

Big Tech and enterprise-acquired platforms occupy the top seven positions, scoring 55 to 65. The stratification is driven by two factors: breadth of compliance certifications (which large organizations can afford to obtain and maintain) and volume of publicly documented security architecture. This does not indicate that higher-ranked platforms are free from critical vulnerabilities. Vertex AI, Copilot Studio, and Agentforce all disclosed critical CVEs during the evaluation window.

The widest single-platform gap in the index: Vertex AI (65) to Langflow (30) — a 35-point spread across platforms that serve overlapping market segments.

03

Key Finding: Compliance Badges Do Not Predict Security Posture

On March 24, 2026, malicious code began executing in production environments around the world. The source: LiteLLM versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8, distributed through PyPI. Within three hours, the compromised packages had been downloaded 47,000 times.

LiteLLM held both SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certifications, issued through Delve, a compliance automation provider. The threat actor group TeamPCP, linked to LAPSUS$, did not need to bypass either certification. The attack exploited a supply chain vector that compliance audits do not examine.

The downstream exposure was severe. According to public PyPI dependency data, 2,337 packages depend on LiteLLM. 88% use unpinned version specifications, meaning they automatically pulled the compromised release. Wiz reported LiteLLM present in 36% of all cloud environments they monitor. Among platforms in this index, CrewAI and tools within the LangChain ecosystem list LiteLLM as a direct dependency.

This Was Not an Isolated Case

Zapier disclosed two security incidents within the evaluation window: a repository breach in March 2025 and a supply chain compromise of its npm SDK in November 2025 affecting 425 packages. Zapier holds SOC 2 and maintains a HackerOne program. Its incident disclosure — immediate, detailed, and directed to all affected users — was the strongest in the index. But the incidents occurred regardless.

Certifications attest to the existence of security processes at a point in time. They do not prevent attacks through vectors those processes were not designed to cover. Procurement decisions that weight certification status as a primary trust signal may be mispricing actual risk.

04

Supply Chain: The Emerging Attack Surface

The March 2026 attack chain was methodical. The threat actor compromised a security scanner first (Trivy, March 19), then a compliance tool (Checkmarx KICS, March 23), then an AI infrastructure library (LiteLLM, March 24). Each step exploited trust. The tools designed to detect compromises were themselves compromised.

These incidents are not occurring in a regulatory vacuum. In January 2026, NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation issued a Request for Information on AI agent security evaluation methodologies, receiving 932 public comments before the March deadline. The federal government is actively seeking frameworks for assessing exactly the kind of risk this report documents.

Security teams assessing AI agent platforms cannot stop at the platform boundary. The dependency tree is the actual attack surface. A platform with zero CVEs can inherit critical risk from a single compromised upstream library.

n8n disclosed 12+ CVEs over the trailing twelve months, including two at CVSS 9.9 or above. Five or more were sandbox bypass vulnerabilities in the same blocklist-based architecture — a structural pattern, not isolated bugs. The migration to Pyodide in v2.0 is an architectural acknowledgment of this limitation.

Pipedream's VM isolation has produced zero public CVEs. Its Workday acquisition raises roadmap questions, but the security architecture stands on its own. The correlation between sandbox quality and CVE count is the clearest signal in this dataset.

06

CISA KEV: What Active Exploitation Means for Enterprise

Inclusion in the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog means a vulnerability has been confirmed as actively exploited in the wild. Two platforms in this index carry that designation.

n8n — CVE-2025-68613

CVSS 9.9. Added to CISA KEV March 2026. FCEB patch deadline: March 25, 2026. Allows authenticated users to execute arbitrary code with n8n process privileges. Censys identified over 100,000 potentially vulnerable instances at disclosure.

Langflow — CVE-2025-3248

CVSS 9.8. Added to CISA KEV May 2025. The Flodrix botnet exploited this across an estimated 1,000+ instances. Approximately 12 months elapsed between initial disclosure and KEV listing. Langflow changed ownership three times during this window (Logspace, DataStax, IBM), raising questions about vulnerability response continuity during acquisition transitions.

Federal agencies subject to Binding Operational Directive 22-01 must remediate KEV-listed vulnerabilities within specified deadlines. For private sector teams, KEV listing is among the strongest available signals that a vulnerability is being actively targeted.

Containment (C) at 4.9/10 is the industry's structural weakness. Platforms have invested in compliance documentation (V) and data governance (D) — the dimensions procurement teams ask about — but underinvested in runtime isolation that limits blast radius when exploitation succeeds.

Resilience (R) with its 4-to-20 range is the most effective single dimension for platform differentiation. AutoGen's 20/20 reflects zero CVEs combined with MSRC's formal coverage commitment (December 2025). Flowise's 4/20 reflects six CVEs in a single March 2026 cluster, including two at CVSS 9.8.

Methodology: Framework v0.3.1

VERDICT Framework v0.3.1 evaluates platforms across seven dimensions, with a maximum Layer 0 score of 85. E (Effectiveness) requires behavioral testing and is excluded from current evaluations.

Data Sources

CVE databases (NVD, MITRE), CISA KEV catalog, GitHub Security Advisories, official Terms of Service, privacy policies, security documentation, compliance certifications, and community-reported incidents. No vendor account, free trial, or internal access is used.

Scoring Principles

Silence is scored. If a vendor does not document a capability, that absence is recorded as zero. An undocumented data retention policy and a confirmed absence of one receive the same score. Procurement teams cannot rely on capabilities that are not publicly verifiable.

Evaluation Layers

Layer 0 — Public Documentation: All publicly available information. This report uses Layer 0 exclusively.

Layer 1 — Behavioral Testing: Free tier. 30 executions per scenario, 95% confidence. Not yet active.

Layer C — Live Monitoring: Continuous. New CVEs trigger immediate score updates. Active for all platforms.

09

Bias Disclosure and Limitations

VERDICT's evaluation tooling uses Claude, developed by Anthropic. Anthropic competes with several evaluated vendors, including OpenAI and Google. This dependency is disclosed explicitly. Mitigations: all scoring criteria are predetermined in Framework v0.3.1; all data sources are public and verifiable; results are reviewed for consistency before publication.

Layer 0 is limited to public documentation. Strong security practices that are not publicly documented will not be reflected in scores. This is by design: undocumented security is operationally invisible to prospective customers.

Q2 2026 Outlook

Three developments will shape the AI agent security landscape in Q2 2026:

Microsoft Zero Trust Assessment for AI. Announced March 2026, scheduled for summer release. This will be the first major vendor-provided self-assessment tool specifically for AI agent deployments. VERDICT will evaluate whether it addresses the structural gaps identified in this report — particularly supply chain and sandbox architecture.

NIST AI Agent Security Guidelines. NIST CAISI received 932 public submissions to its RFI on AI agent security evaluation methodologies. This input may inform forthcoming guidance on how organizations assess agent platform risk. VERDICT's framework and dataset are available as reference material for this process.

VERDICT Layer 1 Activation. Q2 will mark the beginning of behavioral testing for platforms offering free tiers. The E (Effectiveness) dimension, currently excluded, will produce the first statistically validated performance data across platforms — 30 runs per scenario, 95% confidence intervals.

The next edition of this report, VERDICT-RPT-2026-Q2, is scheduled for publication in July 2026.
